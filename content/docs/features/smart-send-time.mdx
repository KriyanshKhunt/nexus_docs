title: Smart Send-Time
description: AI-powered delivery timing — reach each user at their peak engagement hour.
---

**Smart send-time** learns when each subscriber opens, reads, or clicks notifications, then delays delivery to their **peak local hour**.

## Enable on a channel node

In the workflow canvas, toggle **AI Send Time** on any channel node (Email, SMS, Push, etc.).

Excluded automatically: In-App, Webhook, scheduled triggers, and workflows with a Delivery Window node.

## How the model works

1. Nexus tracks `OPENED`, `READ`, and `CLICKED` events per subscriber (60-day lookback)
2. Builds an hourly engagement score grid (day-of-week × hour) in **subscriber local timezone**
3. Stores scores in Redis (refreshed every 6 hours via cron)
4. On delivery, worker delays until the next occurrence of the optimal hour

If insufficient data exists, fallback hour is **10:00 local time**.

## Dashboard visibility

Open a subscriber profile in the dashboard to view the **engagement heatmap** — a 24×7 grid showing when they interact.

## Example

| Subscriber | Timezone | Peak hour | Behavior |
|------------|----------|-----------|----------|
| Alex | `America/New_York` | 2 PM Tue | Order confirmation delayed until Tuesday 2 PM ET |
| Yuki | `Asia/Tokyo` | 8 PM | Marketing email waits until 8 PM JST |

Pass timezone on trigger to keep scores accurate:

```ts
recipients: [{ externalId: 'user_1', timezone: 'America/Chicago' }]
```

## Lifecycle logging

Delayed sends record `SMART_TIMING_HOLD` in the audit lifecycle with `optimalHour`, `delayMs`, and `timezone`.
