**BYOP (Bring Your Own Provider)** is the foundation of Nexus Signal. We orchestrate delivery; **you** own the carrier relationships and pay providers directly.

## What Nexus does

- Visual workflow builder and template engine
- Smart routing, failover, and circuit breaking across **your** provider accounts
- Subscriber preferences, topics, and inbox APIs
- Analytics, audit logs, and cost tracking

## What Nexus does not do

- Resell SMS or email bandwidth
- Mark up per-message fees
- Lock you into a single carrier

## Supported provider layers

Configure one or more providers per channel in **Integrations**, then set **traffic weights** (e.g. 70% SendGrid, 30% Resend for email).

See the full [provider catalog](/docs/platform/integrations/providers).

## Encrypted credentials

Provider API keys are stored encrypted in your organization's `providerKeys` vault. They are decrypted only at dispatch time inside workers.

## AI keys are BYOP too

AI content generation uses **your** OpenAI or Anthropic API key (`openai_ai`, `anthropic_ai` integrations). Nexus does not proxy AI calls through a shared key.

## Why this matters

| Traditional platforms | Nexus Signal |
|----------------------|--------------|
| Bundled SMS/email with markup | You pay Twilio/SendGrid directly |
| Hard to switch providers | Weighted routing + failover across vendors |
| Opaque delivery costs | [Cost analytics](/docs/platform/features/cost-analytics) per workflow |
